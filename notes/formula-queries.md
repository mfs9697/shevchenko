# Formula and source queries

## V. Yu. Marina, “A Hybrid Approach to Calibrating a Multiscale Constitutive Model”

Source pages: 9–14 of the MPTM 2026 Book of Abstracts, Part 2.

1. **Equation (6): `C_k` or `C_{44,k}`?** The printed equation uses `C_k` in two denominators, while the surrounding definitions introduce `C_{11,k}`, `C_{12,k}`, and `C_{44,k}`. The pilot preserves `C_k` because the intended correction cannot be proved from the PDF.
2. **Equation (9): compressed notation.** The symbols involving `\Psi'`, `\psi`, and the time derivative of the subelement irreversible-strain tensor are visually compressed. The transcription is plausible but should be checked against the source file.
3. **Equation (23): `F(b)` corrected to `F(A)`.** The PDF prints `F(b)` in the first fraction, but defines `F(A)` and uses `F(A)` consistently in Eq. (24). The LaTeX pilot uses `F(A)` and treats the source form as a typographical error.
4. **Equation (28): number restored.** The prose refers to “the integral in (28),” but the displayed relation `d\phi=e_p\,dp` is unnumbered in the PDF. The pilot numbers it as Eq. (28).
5. **Faint accents and averaging marks.** Several overbars, averages, and derivative marks were reconstructed from context. These need a final author-level verification.
