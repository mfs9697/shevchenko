# Editorial decisions for reports 17–23

Source pages: 32–39 of the MPTM 2026 Book of Abstracts, Part 2.

1. **Multilayer dynamics.** The duplicated source wording `розглянуто сформульовано постановки` was rewritten as a single grammatically complete statement. The author’s term `регуляційно-спектральний метод` was retained because it functions as the stated name of the method; it was not silently replaced by `регуляризаційно-спектральний`.
2. **SH-wave report.** The title begins with the truncated source word `ОЛЬ`; it was restored to `РОЛЬ`. `повздовжні P-хвилі` was normalized to `поздовжні P-хвилі`.
3. **Experimental equipment.** The two source photographs were converted into scalable TikZ schematics that retain the essential geometry of the vertical water tank, suspended test frame, long shock tube, supports, and auxiliary measuring equipment.
4. **FGM thermoviscoplasticity.** Institutional naming, grammar, and the description of the local deformation and creep diagrams were normalized. No constitutive equations or experimental assumptions were added beyond those stated in the source.
5. **FGM creep.** The Reuss isostress approach and the scalar self-consistent model were retained as the two reported homogenization options; the conclusion that Reuss provides an upper estimate of creep strain was preserved.
6. **Random through-thickness shell damage.** Repetitive and grammatically incomplete sentences were consolidated while preserving the experimental variables, loading modes, comparison with finite-element analysis, and the stated purpose of durability prediction.
7. **Partially permeable piezoelectric crack.** The source places the crack on `x_2=0`, although the problem is posed in the `(x_1,x_3)` plane and the material interface is defined by `x_3=0`. This was corrected to `x_3=0`. The scale factors `10^{-5}` and `10^{-6}`, dielectric relation, finite-element types, and permeability conclusion were preserved.
