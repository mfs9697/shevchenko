# Editorial decisions for reports 10–16

Source pages: 23–31 of the MPTM 2026 Book of Abstracts, Part 2.

1. **Ti–CFRP/GFRP wear report.** The title was corrected from “Simulation Wear Resistance…” to “Simulation of the Wear Resistance…”. Fragmented and duplicated English sentences were rebuilt without changing the reported 15–30% thickness relation or the claimed order-of-magnitude reduction in vibration amplitude. The original two-panel micrograph is preserved.
2. **Drill-string report.** Ukrainian grammar and terminology were normalized. The quoted source phrase “rigid model of drag and torque” was rendered as a three-dimensional drag-and-torque model that accounts for the bending stiffness of the drill string, consistent with the stated curved flexible-rod theory.
3. **Fluid-separated plate.** Equations (1)–(6) were reconstructed from raster equation objects. The symbols and equation numbering follow the rendered source. In Eq. (4), the source visibly gives `B_n = ψ_n|_Γ = const`; this notation is retained. In Eq. (5), the denominator is transcribed as `[aω²a_nm − ξ_nm d_nm]`.
4. **Endochronic tubular element.** The displayed source kernel prints `ρ(z)=C_i exp(−α_i z)` without a summation sign, while the text later identifies multiple indexed pairs `C_i, α_i`. It was therefore reconstructed as the exponential sum `ρ(z)=Σ_i C_i exp(−α_i z)`. The stress-increment equations retain the source notation with a common coefficient `α`.
5. **Pavlenko–Manevich method.** A malformed citation `[1, 2, ]` was corrected to `[1, 2]`; language and affiliation formatting were normalized.
6. **Sandwich shell with holes.** The repeated author name in reference 3 is present in the source and has been preserved rather than silently guessed. The title and examples were rendered in explicitly civilian “launch-vehicle / space-technology” terminology without changing the mechanics problem.
7. **Ribbed elliptic shell.** The title’s obvious initial typo `РО` was corrected to `ПРО`. The original structural sketch is preserved as a raster figure.
